Landmarks of Derry

The Guildhall

The Guildhall is one of the centerpieces of Derry and is home to the Derry City and Strabane District Council, where they hold regular council meetings.

The building was first designed and built in 1890 by John Guy Ferguson, which replaced the old Market House (which was destroyed in the Seige of Derry).
The original building was destroyed in a fire in 1908 and subsequently was redesigned and rebuilt by Matthew Robinson, who took inspiration from the Elizabeth Tower in London.

The Guildhall is host to many important events and guests, most notably, the US President President Bill Clinton as well as John Hume.
