Landmarks of Derry

The City Walls

The historic Derry walls were first built between 1613 and 1619, and their original purpose was to protect Scottish and English planters that had moved to Ulster at the time. The walls have survived a number of conflicts, most notably, The Siege of Derry and The Troubles. During these times of crisis, the Apprentice Boys (A Protestant fraternal society) would close the gates to the city, which consisted of:

  • Ferryquay Gate
  • Shipquay Gate
  • Bishop's Gate
  • Butcher's Gate
  • New Gate
  • Castle Gate
  • Magazine Gate

The walls are approximately 1 mile in circumference and are entirely accessible on foot. The walls also house a variety of 22 cannons from the 16th, 17th and the 18th century, as well as boasting incredible views over the city. This is a must see landmark for any visitor in Derry!
