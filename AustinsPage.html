Landmarks of Derry

Austins Department Store

The world's oldest independent department store. Austins was opened in 1830 and even predates its other world famous counterparts Macys or Harrods.

The photo of Austins to the right shows the store's new Edwardian design, which was re-built after a devastating fire occured in the original store in 1904. The new design was constructed by Matthew Robinson, who had also designed the reconstructed Guildhall, as well as other notable sites such as the Craigavon Bridge and the Rosemount T-shirt factory (a major business in Derry).
The building consists of 5 floors, with an open stair well running through each floor. It is topped with a fine glass atrium and a top floor restaurant.

Unfortunately, after 186 years, Austins was permanently closed down on the 8th March 2016 due to liquidation issues with the owners, Hassonzender. Although not open for business, the building still remains as a beautifal landmark in the heart of Derry and is a worthwile stop for any visitor to see!
