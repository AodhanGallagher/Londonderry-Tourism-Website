Landmarks of Derry

The Peace Bridge

Opening in 2011, The Peace Bridge is a cycle and foot bridge that stands at 771ft long and spans across the River Foyle. The bridge connects the two halves of the city, from the waterside (with an entrance at Ebrington Square) to the city side (near the city centre).

The bridge was intended to improve relations between the mostly unionist waterside and the mostly nationalist cityside by improving access to the two areas. Furthermore, the bridge's design features two sloping pillars that symbolise a coming-together of the two communities.
