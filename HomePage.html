Landmarks of Derry

Welcome to the City of Derry


A city steeped in history, Derry has a lot to offer. From being the only remaining completely walled city in Ireland to being the UK's first City of Culture in 2013, there's plenty to see and do.

Below are some of the main landmarks that highlight Derry's rich history and are really worth a visit!

The City Walls

The Derry Walls define the old town quarter at the heart of the modern city. Within these walls is a network of streets, full of character and charm.
The Peace Bridge

The Derry Peace Bridge over the River Foyle bridges a 400 year old physical and political gap between two sides of a once, bitterly divided community.
Austins

The world's oldest independant department store. It survived the great Irish famine, two world wars, the partition of the island and the Ulster Troubles.
The Guildhall

A warm welcome awaits you at the Guildhall, one of Derrys most recognisable landmarks that has been at the heart of city life since 1890.
